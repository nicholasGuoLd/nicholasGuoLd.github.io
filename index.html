The Different among BRD, MRD, PRD

Posted on 2017-09-21 | In Product knowledge , Product manager

What is the meaning of these three?

  • BRD
  • MRD
  • PRD

Simple explaination:

  • BRD: that is Business Requirement Document, which is the head of one human;
  • MRD: that is Market Requirement Document, which is the body of one human;
  • PRD: that is Product Requirement Documenet, which is the heart of one human;
  • Large - High ==> BRD: a new product; more important and has a complex development in the future;
  • Middle - Medium ==> MRD: need to achieve a new function or system adding in a normal product;
  • Small - Low ==> PRD: it is detailed one; this proposal has been built and need to provide the details about product; optimise the function and UI;

Who cares and how to implement:

Business Requirement Document (BRD)

  • Who: Boss, CEO or Project manager;
  • How:

  1. What product do we do? (defination + competitive advantages)

  2. What resources do we need? (labours + schedual + cost + relationship = market position)

  3. What is the look like in future? (revenue)

Tips: Business model; Benefit model; Resource model; Market advantage;

Market Requirement Document (MRD)

  • Who: Business man, Operating team or Marketing team;
  • How:

  1. Define the style of customers and accumulate resources;

  2. Communicate with cooperate company –> service model; self position; increase benefit;

  3. Target customers –> business: extend resources; market: product protection; operation: product promotion;

Tips: Product model, Service model, Operating model, Marketing direction and User model;

Product Requirement Document (PRD)

  • Who: Project team, Develop team, Testing team, Planning team, Experience team;
  • How

  1. What is the product look like? (UI, UE, Original draft, Flow diagram)

  2. How to achieve it? (plan schedual, humen and resources; use tools like VISIO)

  3. How to audit it? (requirement audit –> black-box audit, white-box audit and even grey-box audit –> optimising requirement: users data, UI optimising and customers position –> experience staff: start product line and collect users information)

Tips: User interface, Function requirement, Test requirement, Experience requirement, Effective and effecient.
